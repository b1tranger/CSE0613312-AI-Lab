2. Complex (Composite) Data Structures 
In a broader programming sense, "complex data types" refer to composite structures that group multiple values together. 

- <mark>Lists: Ordered, mutable sequences typically used for collections of related items. </mark>
- <mark>Tuples: Ordered, immutable sequences often used for fixed data like coordinates. </mark>
- <mark>Dictionaries: Key-value pairs used for mapping unique keys to specific values. </mark>
